  • It seems like there’s a Thai food restaurant on every corner in New York. However, many of these establishments serve mild and bland food (*not enough chillies and/or fish sauce). Since I arrived in NY in 2011, I've ventured through the five boroughs to seek the most authentic Thai dishes. I'm not talking about Pad Thai or Green Curry– I'm talking about fermented fish, blood noodles, and gizzards! So here is a list of my go-to places in town. Spread the love, and always remember to add more pepper

  • Pure Thai Cookhouse

    PURE Thai Cookhouse is a genuine Thai shophouse style restaurant in NYC’s Hell’s Kitchen that features homemade noodles from a prized family recipe. Pure also specializes in signature dishes you would find from the many vendors and shophouses from the farmlands of Phayao, in the North to the floating markets of Ratchaburi in Central Thailand, to the oceans and fishing villages of Krabi in the South.

    Recommended Dishes: Yum Nuea (Beef salad), Guaw Tiew Nuea (Beef soup), Guaw Tiew Tom Yum (Tom yum noodle soup)

    Check out their website here »

  • Elmhurst Khao Mung Gai

    At Eim Khao Mun Kai, the only dish on the menu is khao man gai, a sort of punkified cover version of Hainanese chicken rice. The chicken is organic, poached whole with ginger, and comes with gizzards and liver.

    Recommeded dishes: Chicken and Rice

    Click here to read more reviews on Yelp »

  • Zab Elee

    Modern, compact Thai eatery with a focus on unusual, spicy dishes from the Northeastern Isan region.

    Recommended Dishes: Khao Tom (Rice porridge), Soup Nor Mai (Bamboo with northeastern spices), Yum Talae (Seafood salad)

    Check out their website here »

  • Sripaphai

    Long lines often form for traditional hot & spicy Thai dishes at this renowned spot.

    Recommended dishes: Pla Neung Manao (Steamed Fish in Lime and Garlic) Gang Tai Pla (Fish stomach curry with bamboo shoots) Guay Tiew Ped (Duck Noodle Soup)

    Check out their website here »

  • Khao Kang

    Perhaps “restaurant” is an exaggeration. There is no menu, no wait service. Food is made in advance, throughout the day, and presented cafeteria style. Do not look askance. The steam-table joint is based on an honorable and beloved class of establishment in Thailand known as raan khao kaeng, or rice-curry shop, sometimes just a sidewalk stall with giant pots and a few plastic chairs that patrons circle and stalk.

    Recommended Dishes: EVERYTHING! Don't skip out dessert either

    Click here to read more reviews on Yelp »

  • Som Tum Der

    Smartly decorated Thai restaurant presenting regional Isan cuisine via shareable plates.

    Recommended Dishes: Larb Woonsen (Glass noodles with gizzards in northeastern spices), Som Tum Plara (Papaya salad with fermented fish), Tom Zabb (Hot & soup with pork ribs, enoki mushrooms, lemongrass, and ginger)

    Check out their website here »
